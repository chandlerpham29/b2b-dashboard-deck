03 / PURPOSE
Why this dashboard exists

Purpose of the B2B Overview

The B2B Business Overview dashboard is the central QBR reporting surface for ELSA's sales leadership and regional teams. It consolidates HubSpot CRM data, revenue bookings, pipeline health, and goal attainment into a single, always-fresh view — replacing manual spreadsheet decks and ensuring everyone works from the same source of truth.

Key questions it answers
  • Are we on track to hit our targets — or already behind?
  • Where are we right now — current bookings, pipeline, and coverage?
  • How did we perform last quarter — deals closed, trials completed, win rate?
  • Which teams or regions are ahead or behind on their goals?
Data sources
  • HubSpot — deals, pipeline stages, and most operational metrics
  • Quarterly goals — updated manually when targets are set (fixed each quarter)
  • GRR — sourced from the GRR dashboard, not HubSpot
04 / STREAMLIT
Introduction

Why Streamlit Instead of Looker & HubSpot?

Comparing all three options side by side — pros, cons, and why Streamlit is the best fit for our QBR dashboard.

Streamlit
Pros
  • Multi-source integration — HubSpot, databases, APIs, spreadsheets, and CSV in one dashboard
  • Advanced analytics — Python-based custom logic, forecasting, segmentation, and AI insights
  • Fast prototyping — build, test, and iterate quickly without heavy BI modeling
  • Scalable — add features over time as business needs evolve
Cons
  • Less plug-and-play — not as easy for non-technical users as HubSpot dashboards
Looker
Pros
  • Enterprise BI — strong for governed reporting at scale
  • Standardized metrics — reusable data models and shared definitions
Cons
  • Heavier setup — more effort than Streamlit or HubSpot to get started
  • Requires data modeling — dashboards aren't useful until models are built
  • Less flexible — slower for rapid custom dashboard app development
  • Overkill for ops dashboards — more complex than needed for a customized QBR view
HubSpot
Pros
  • Easy for CRM users — quick to create basic dashboards without technical setup
  • Real-time data — live deal and pipeline information from our main CRM
Cons
  • Limited custom logic — less flexibility for advanced analytics
  • Single-source focused — less suitable when pulling from multiple external systems
  • Restricted layouts — fewer visualization and layout options
  • CRM reporting only — better for CRM views than fully customized analytics
Why we selected Streamlit — Looker is built for enterprise BI but too heavy for a fast, customized QBR workflow. HubSpot is easy and real-time but can't combine multiple data sources or support advanced custom logic. Streamlit gives us the flexibility to integrate HubSpot with our own metrics, iterate quickly, and scale — making it the best fit for this dashboard.
05 / BEHAVIOUR
Good to know upfront

Dashboard Behaviour & Common Challenges

How the data works
  • All operational data comes from HubSpot — synced roughly every hour
  • The dashboard refreshes each time you open it or click Rerun
  • Quarterly goal targets are updated manually when leadership sets them
  • GRR is pulled from the GRR dashboard, not HubSpot
Deals: ~1h lag Pipeline: ~1h lag Goals: manual update
Challenges as a first-time user
  • Date filter confusion — each tab uses close date, create date, or subscription date differently
  • Data lag (~1 hour) — for real-time deal status, cross-check HubSpot directly
  • Numbers vs. old spreadsheets — metric logic is standardized here; this dashboard is the source of truth
  • Access issues — first-time users need login credentials; contact the Data team if blocked
Tip — Data looks stale? Hit Rerun in the top-right. Streamlit may cache results for up to 10 minutes within a session.
06 / ACCESS
Getting started

How to Access & Use the Dashboard

① Open
Log in to Snowflake → navigate to Streamlit Apps or use the direct link shared by the data team.
② Filter
The two most important filters are Date (Quarter) and Team. Use Quarter to set the reporting period and Team to drill into a specific region. Additional filters appear per tab as needed.
③ Navigate
There are 5 tabs, each with a slightly different date context — close date, create date, or subscription date — which is why each tab carries its own dedicated filter set.

Metrics Definition

TabMetricDefinition
QBR Scorecard ACV Booked Annual Contract Value that has been secured in fiscal period (based on the Deal Close Date), coming from new contracts, renewal of expiring contracts, or incremental upsell of active contracts.
New ACVIncremental New Annual Contract Value that has been secured in fiscal period, coming from new contracts, cross-sells, mid-term upsells, and any transactional deals that don't have renewal base.
Renewal ACVRetained Annual Contract Value that has been secured in fiscal period, coming from renewal of subscriptions expiring in current, previous (late renewals), or next (early renewals) fiscal quarter. Renewal ACV includes flat renewals, renewals w/ upsell, renewals w/ downsell.
GRR (%)Percentage of existing subscription-based recurring revenue that successfully retained over a period of time, ignoring any new sales, upsells, or cross-sells. Gross Retention is calculated based on Subscription Dates, ignoring Close Date of renewals. Only renewals within 30 days from Subscription End Date included in the GRR balance.
New Pipeline Created ($M)Total value of qualified new opportunities (New Business Pipeline, including deal types: One-Time, New Contract, Cross-Sell, Mid-Term Upsell) created during the fiscal period. Based on the deals creation date and ignores Close Date and Status.
# of New Deal Created (SAO)Number of new Deals (SAO) created during the fiscal period (New Business Pipeline, including deal types: One-Time, New Contract, Cross-Sell, Mid-Term Upsell). Based on the deals creation date and ignores Close Date and Status.
# of New Trial CompletedNumber of deals exited Trial stage during the fiscal period (New Business Pipeline, including deal types: One-Time, New Contract, Cross-Sell, Mid-Term Upsell). Based on Trial Completion Date and ignores Close Date and Status.
# of New Deal ClosedNumber of new deals that have been successfully closed won during the fiscal period (New Business Pipeline, including deal types: One-Time, New Contract, Cross-Sell, Mid-Term Upsell). Based on the deal Close Date.
Average ACV per New Deal ($K)Average Annual Contract Value generated per new customer (Deal Type = One-Time, New Contract, Cross-Sell, Mid-Term Upsell), calculated as total New ACV divided by the number of new customers acquired in the fiscal period.

ACVAnnual Contract Value secured in the fiscal period, calculated by summing Amount (USD) from closed-won deals. ACV can be broken down by key segments such as region, team, deal type, business type, or customer segment.
Won CompaniesNumber of unique companies with at least one successfully closed-won deal during the fiscal period. Termination deals/accounts are excluded from the calculation.
Active ACV Snapshot Active Customers by Quarter Number of unique customers who hold an active subscription as of the respective quarter.

Pipeline Creation Pipeline Created Total pipeline value generated within a specific period, based on the deal create date.

Conversion RateCalculated as the percentage of pipeline created within a specific period that converted into won bookings.

# of Deals by Deal SizeNumber of closed deals segmented by predefined deal size categories (small, medium, and large) within a specific period.
ACV by Deal SizeTotal ACV from closed deals segmented by predefined deal size categories within a specific period.
